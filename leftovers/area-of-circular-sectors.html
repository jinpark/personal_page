Area of Circular Sectors 2016

Question 1

Rolly wishes to secure his dog with an 8-foot rope to a square shed that is 16 feet on each side. His preliminary drawings are shown.

Which of these arrangements gives the dog the greater area to roam, and by how many square feet?

a. I, by $8\pi$

b. I, by $6\pi$

c. II, by $4\pi$

d. II, by $8\pi$

e.II, by $10\pi$

Question 2

Two circles of radius $2$ are centered at $(2,0)$ and at $(0,2)$. What is the area of the intersection of the interiors of the two circles?

a. $\pi-2$

b. $\dfrac{\pi}{2}$

c. $\dfrac{\pi\sqrt{3}}{3}$

d. $2(\pi-2)$

e.$\pi$

Question 3

A circular grass plot $12$ feet in diameter is cut by a straight gravel path $3$ feet wide, one edge of which passes through the center of the plot. The number of square feet in the remaining grass area is

a. $36\pi-34$

b. $30\pi-15$

c. $36\pi-33$

d. $35\pi-9\sqrt{3}$

e.$30\pi-9\sqrt{3}$

Question 4

The figure shown is called a trefoil and is constructed by drawing circular sectors about sides of the congruent equilateral triangles. What is the area of a trefoil whose horizontal base has length 2?

a. $\dfrac{1}{3}\pi+ \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$

b. $\dfrac{2}{3}\pi$

c. $\dfrac{2}{3}\pi+ \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4}$

d. $\dfrac{2}{3}\pi+ \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$

e.$\dfrac{2}{3}\pi+ \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$
