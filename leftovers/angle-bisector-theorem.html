Angle Bisector Theorem

Question 1

Triangle $ABC$ has a right angle at $B$, $AB = 1$, and $BC = 2$. The bisector of $\angle BAC$ meets $\overline{BC}$ at $D$. What is $BD$?

a. $\dfrac{\sqrt{3}-1}{2}$

b. $\dfrac{\sqrt{5} - 1}{2}$

c. $\dfrac{\sqrt{5}+1}{2}$

d. $\dfrac{\sqrt{6} + \sqrt{2}}{2}$

e.$2\sqrt{3}-1$

Question 2

Right triangle $ABC$ has hypotenuse $\overline{AB}$ of length $5$ and one leg $\overline{AC}$ of length $3$. The bisector of angle $A$ meets the opposite side at $A_1$. A second right triangle $PQR$ is then constructed with hypotenuse of length $PQ=A_1B$ and one leg of length $PR=A_1C$. The bisector of angle $P$ meets the opposite side at $P_1$. What is $PP_1$?

a. $\displaystyle\dfrac{3\sqrt{6}}{4}$

b. $\displaystyle\dfrac{3\sqrt{5}}{4}$

c. $\displaystyle\dfrac{3\sqrt{3}}{4}$

d. $\displaystyle\dfrac{3\sqrt{2}}{2}$

e.$\displaystyle\dfrac{15\sqrt{2}}{16}$

Question 3

In $\triangle ABC$, point $D$ is on $\overline{BC}$ and point $F$ is on $\overline{AB}$. Segments $\overline{AD}$ and $\overline{CF}$ intersect at $G$. Furthermore, $\angle ABC=30^{\circ}=\angle CAD$ and $\angle ACF=15^{\circ}=\angle BCF$. How many of the following equalities are correct?

(i) $AB = AC$

(ii) $AD = CD$

(iii) $AF = AG$

(iv) $AF = BF$

a. $0$

b. $1$

c. $2$

d. $3$

e.$4$

Question 4

A circle centered at $O$ has radius 1 and contains the point $A$. Segment $\overline{AB}$ is tangent to the circle at $A$ such that $OC = \dfrac{51}{100}$ and $\angle ABC = \angle CBO$. What is $AB$?

a. $2\sqrt3$

b. $\dfrac{49\sqrt2}{20}$

c. $\dfrac72$

d. $\dfrac{6\sqrt{17}}{7}$

e.$\dfrac{\sqrt{51}}{2}$

Question 5

In $\triangle ABC$, points $D$ and $E$ are located on $\overline{BC}$ such that $\overline{AD}$ is an angle bisector and $\overline{AE}$ is a median. Furthermore, $AB = 15$, $AC = 21$, and $DE = 2$. What is $BC$?

a. $18$

b. $21$

c. $24$

d. $27$

e.$30$
