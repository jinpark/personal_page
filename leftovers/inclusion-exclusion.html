Inclusion Exclusion

Question 1

Andrew wrote all the odd numbers from $1$ to $2011$ on a board and then Bob erased all the multiples of $3$. How many numbers were left on the board?

a. $335$

b. $336$

c. $671$

d. $1005$

e.$1006$

Question 2

There are some balls in a raffle bucket. One positive integer is written on each ball; all the integers are different, and each is divisible by either $6$ or $7$. A number divisible by $6$ is written on exactly $30$ balls, a number divisible by $7$ is written on exactly $20$ balls and a number divisible by $42$ is written on exactly $10$ balls. How many balls are in the bucket?

a. $30$

b. $40$

c. $53$

d. $54$

e.$60$

Question 3

Everyone attending an international conference of mathematicians speaks either English, Russian, or Chinese. A total of $53$ people speak English, $39$ speak Russian, and $44$ speak Chinese. There are $31$ people who speak at least two of the three languanges, including $12$ who speak all three. How many mathematicians are attending the conference?

a. $81$

b. $93$

c. $100$

d. $117$

e.$136$

Question 4

How many ways are there of choosing $3$ different numbers in increasing order from $\{1, 2, 3, \ldots, 10\}$ so that no two of the numbers are consecutive?

a. $20$

b. $48$

c. $56$

d. $54$

e.$72$

Question 5

How many triangles with positive area are there whose vertices are points in the $xy$-plane whose coordinates are integers $(x,y)$ satisfying $1 \leq x \leq 4$ and $1 \leq y \leq 4$?

a. $496$

b. $500$

c. $512$

d. $516$

e.$560$
