Vieta's Formulas

Question 1

The quadratic equation $x^2+mx+n=0$ has roots that are twice those of $x^2+px+m=0$, and none of $m$, $n$ and $p$ is zero. What is the value of $n/p$?

a. $1$

b. $2$

c. $4$

d. $8$

e.$16$

Question 2

Let $a$ and $b$ be the roots of the equation $x^2 + px + q = 0$. An equation with $a^2 + ab$ and $ab + b^2$ as roots is

a. $x^2 + p^2x + p^2q = 0$

b. $x^2 - p^2x - p^2q = 0$

c. $x^2 +p^2x -p^2q = 0$

d. $x^2 - p^2x + p^2q = 0$

e.$x^2-p^2x+pq^2 = 0$

Question 3

If three of the roots of $x^4+ax^2+bx+c=0$ are $1$, $2$, and $3$, then the value of $a+c$ is:

a. $35$

b. $24$

c. $-12$

d. $-61$

e.$-63$

Question 4

The cubic equation $x^3 - 18x^2 + ax - b = 0$ has three positive real roots. Which of the following intervals contains the largest possible value of $b$?

a. $[1, 10]$

b. $[10, 50]$

c. $[50, 100]$

d. $[100, 200]$

e.$[200, 500]$

Question 5

Part of the graph of $f(x) = ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d$ is shown. What is $b$?

a. $-4$

b. $-2$

c. $0$

d. $2$

e.$4$
